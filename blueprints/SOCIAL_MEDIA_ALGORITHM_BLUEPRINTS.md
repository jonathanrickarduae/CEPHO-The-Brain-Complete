## Executive Summary

This document provides comprehensive blueprints for leveraging the algorithms of six major social media platforms. Each blueprint includes the core ranking factors, content optimization strategies, posting cadence, and tactical playbooks designed to work with (not against) each platform's algorithm.

These blueprints are designed for B2B and B2C outreach, brand building, and lead generation as part of the Project Genesis marketing infrastructure.

## Platform 1: LinkedIn

### Algorithm Overview

LinkedIn's algorithm is specifically designed to **prevent content from going viral** and instead prioritizes **relevant professional content** to users based on their interests, connections, and engagement history [1].

**Core Principle:** LinkedIn rewards expertise, knowledge sharing, and meaningful professional engagement over entertainment or viral content.

### Ranking Factors (Priority Order)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Content Quality** | High | Original insights, industry expertise, actionable advice |
| **2. First Hour Engagement** | High | Comments and reactions in first 60 minutes ("Golden Hour") |
| **3. Dwell Time** | High | How long users spend reading your post |
| **4. Network Relevance** | Medium | Connection strength with viewers |
| **5. Topic Authority** | Medium | Consistent posting on specific niche topics |
| **6. Comment Quality** | Medium | Thoughtful comments from relevant professionals |

### Three-Step Algorithm Process

1. **Quality Filtering** - Posts immediately classified as spam, low quality, or high quality
2. **Engagement Testing** - Shown to small sample of audience; strong engagement = wider distribution
3. **Network & Relevance Ranking** - Prioritizes posts from people and topics you engage with most

### Content Optimization Playbook

**What Works:**
- Original insights and industry analysis
- Personal stories with professional lessons
- Actionable advice and frameworks
- Carousel documents (high dwell time)
- Polls (high engagement)
- Text-only posts with clear structure

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree!")
- Excessive hashtags (limit to 3-5)
- Posting more than once per 12 hours
- Tagging unrelated people
- External links in post body (put in comments)

### Posting Cadence

| Frequency | Timing | Content Type |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| 3-5x per week | Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am | Thought leadership |
| 1x per week | Any day | Carousel/Document |
| 1x per week | Wednesday | Poll |
| Daily | Throughout day | Engage on others' posts |

### B2B Lead Generation Tactics

1. **Build Topic Authority** - Post consistently about one niche for 90 days
2. **Engage Before Posting** - Comment on 10-15 posts before publishing your own
3. **Use LinkedIn Articles** - For long-form content that stays discoverable
4. **Leverage Employee Advocacy** - Team members sharing company content
5. **DM Strategy** - Connect → Engage on their content → Personalized message

## Platform 2: X (Twitter)

### Algorithm Overview

X's algorithm prioritizes **recency, engagement velocity, and relevance** to the user's interests. The "For You" feed uses machine learning to surface content from accounts you follow and accounts you might find interesting [2].

**Core Principle:** X rewards timely, engaging content that sparks conversation and gets rapid engagement.

### Ranking Factors (Priority Order)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Engagement Velocity** | Very High | Likes, retweets, replies in first 30 minutes |
| **2. Recency** | High | Newer content prioritized |
| **3. Media Attachments** | High | Images, videos, polls boost visibility |
| **4. Reply Depth** | Medium | Threads with back-and-forth conversation |
| **5. Account Authority** | Medium | Follower count, verification, engagement history |
| **6. Topic Relevance** | Medium | Content matching user's interests |

### Content Optimization Playbook

**What Works:**
- Threads (multi-tweet stories) - 2-3x more engagement
- Images and videos - 150% more retweets
- Polls - High engagement, algorithm boost
- Quote tweets with commentary
- Timely takes on trending topics
- Controversial (but professional) opinions

**What Doesn't Work:**
- External links (suppress reach by 50%+)
- Hashtag overuse (1-2 max)
- Posting without engagement
- Automated/scheduled posts without engagement

### Posting Cadence

| Frequency | Timing | Content Type |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| 3-5x per day | 8am, 12pm, 5pm, 8pm | Short takes, commentary |
| 2-3x per week | Any time | Threads (5-15 tweets) |
| 1x per week | Peak hours | Poll |
| Continuous | Throughout day | Replies and quote tweets |

### Viral Content Formula

1. **Hook** - First line must stop the scroll (curiosity, controversy, value promise)
2. **Thread Structure** - Each tweet must stand alone but build narrative
3. **Call to Action** - End with engagement prompt (retweet, follow, reply)
4. **Self-Reply** - Reply to your own tweet with link (avoids algorithm penalty)

## Platform 3: Instagram

### Algorithm Overview

Instagram uses **multiple algorithms** - one for Feed, one for Stories, one for Reels, and one for Explore. Each has different ranking signals, but all prioritize **engagement, relevance, and relationship strength** [3].

**Core Principle:** Instagram rewards content that keeps users on the platform longer, with Reels currently receiving the highest algorithmic boost.

### Ranking Factors by Format

**Feed Algorithm:**
| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Relationship** | Very High | How often you interact with this account |
| **2. Interest** | High | Predicted interest based on past behavior |
| **3. Timeliness** | Medium | Newer posts prioritized |
| **4. Engagement** | Medium | Likes, comments, saves, shares |

**Reels Algorithm:**
| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Watch Time** | Very High | Percentage of video watched |
| **2. Engagement** | High | Saves and shares weighted highest |
| **3. Audio Trending** | Medium | Using trending sounds boosts reach |
| **4. Completion Rate** | Medium | Videos watched to end |

**Stories Algorithm:**
| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Relationship** | Very High | Accounts you interact with most |
| **2. Viewing History** | High | Stories you consistently watch |
| **3. Engagement** | Medium | Replies, reactions, shares |

### Content Optimization Playbook

**Reels (Highest Priority):**
- 15-30 seconds optimal length
- Hook in first 1-3 seconds
- Use trending audio
- Text overlays for accessibility
- Post 4-7 Reels per week

**Feed Posts:**
- Carousel posts get 1.4x more reach than single images
- Detailed captions (150-300 words) boost engagement
- Post 3-5 feed posts per week

**Stories:**
- Post 5-10 Stories per day
- Use interactive stickers (polls, questions, quizzes)
- Behind-the-scenes content performs well

### Engagement Hierarchy

**Most Valuable to Least:**
1. Saves (signals high value)
2. Shares (signals shareability)
3. Comments (signals conversation)
4. Likes (signals approval)

### Posting Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Best Times |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Reels | 4-7x per week | 9am, 12pm, 7pm |
| Feed | 3-5x per week | 11am, 1pm, 7pm |
| Stories | 5-10x per day | Throughout day |
| Lives | 1-2x per week | Peak hours |

## Platform 4: TikTok

### Algorithm Overview

TikTok's "For You Page" (FYP) algorithm is the most sophisticated content recommendation system, designed to surface content from **any creator** regardless of follower count. The algorithm prioritizes **watch time, engagement, and content relevance** [4].

**Core Principle:** TikTok rewards content that keeps users watching and engaging, giving every video a chance to go viral regardless of account size.

### Ranking Factors (Priority Order)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Watch Time** | Very High | % of video watched, rewatches, loops |
| **2. Engagement** | High | Likes, comments, shares, saves |
| **3. Video Completion** | High | Videos watched to end |
| **4. Content Relevance** | Medium | Hashtags, sounds, captions matching interests |
| **5. Account Activity** | Medium | Posting consistency, engagement with others |
| **6. Device/Account Settings** | Low | Language, location, device type |

### Content Optimization Playbook

**What Works:**
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds (critical)
- 15-60 seconds optimal length
- Trending sounds and effects
- Text overlays
- Face-to-camera content
- Educational/value-driven content
- Pattern interrupts every 3-5 seconds

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Slow intros
- Watermarked content from other platforms
- Low-quality video/audio
- Overly promotional content
- Inconsistent posting

### Video Structure Formula

1. **Hook (0-2 sec)** - Pattern interrupt, question, or bold statement
2. **Setup (2-5 sec)** - Context for what's coming
3. **Value (5-50 sec)** - Main content, tips, story
4. **Loop/CTA (last 2 sec)** - Encourage rewatch or action

### Posting Cadence

| Frequency | Timing | Content Type |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| 1-3x per day | 7am, 12pm, 7pm | Short-form content |
| Daily | Throughout day | Engage with comments |
| Weekly | Any time | Trend participation |

### Hashtag Strategy

- Use 3-5 hashtags per video
- Mix of: 1 broad (#business), 2 niche (#startuptips), 1-2 trending
- Avoid banned or overused hashtags

## Platform 5: YouTube

### Algorithm Overview

YouTube's algorithm optimizes for **watch time and session time** - how long users watch your videos and how long they stay on YouTube after watching. The algorithm surfaces content through Search, Suggested Videos, and Home Feed [5].

**Core Principle:** YouTube rewards content that keeps viewers watching longer and coming back to the platform.

### Ranking Factors (Priority Order)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Watch Time** | Very High | Total minutes watched |
| **2. Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | Very High | % of impressions that result in clicks |
| **3. Audience Retention** | High | % of video watched on average |
| **4. Engagement** | High | Likes, comments, shares, subscribes |
| **5. Session Time** | Medium | How long viewers stay on YouTube after |
| **6. Upload Frequency** | Medium | Consistent posting schedule |

### Content Optimization Playbook

**Thumbnail Optimization:**
- High contrast colors
- Faces with emotion
- Text overlay (3-5 words max)
- Curiosity gap

**Title Optimization:**
- Front-load keywords
- Include numbers when possible
- Create curiosity without clickbait
- 60 characters or less

**Video Structure:**
- Hook in first 30 seconds
- Pattern interrupts every 2-3 minutes
- Clear value proposition
- End screen with next video

### Content Types by Performance

| Type | Watch Time | CTR | Best For |
|------|------------|-----|----------|
| **Tutorials** | High | Medium | SEO, evergreen |
| **Lists** | Medium | High | Discoverability |
| **Stories** | High | Medium | Engagement |
| **Shorts** | Low | Very High | Subscriber growth |

### Posting Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Best Times |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Long-form | 1-2x per week | Consistent day/time |
| Shorts | 3-7x per week | Any time |
| Community Posts | 2-3x per week | Any time |

### YouTube Shorts Strategy

- 15-60 seconds
- Vertical format (9:16)
- Hook in first 2 seconds
- Loop-friendly endings
- Cross-post from TikTok/Reels (remove watermarks)

## Platform 6: Facebook

### Algorithm Overview

Facebook's algorithm prioritizes **meaningful interactions** - content that sparks conversations between friends and family. Business content is deprioritized unless it generates genuine engagement [6].

**Core Principle:** Facebook rewards content that creates meaningful social interactions, especially in Groups and among close connections.

### Ranking Factors (Priority Order)

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **1. Meaningful Interactions** | Very High | Comments, shares, reactions from close connections |
| **2. Content Type** | High | Video > Images > Links > Text |
| **3. Recency** | Medium | Newer content prioritized |
| **4. Engagement Velocity** | Medium | Early engagement signals quality |
| **5. Page/Profile Authority** | Medium | Engagement history, follower quality |

### Content Optimization Playbook

**What Works:**
- Native video (especially Live)
- Content that sparks discussion
- Facebook Groups (highest organic reach)
- Reels (currently boosted)
- User-generated content
- Behind-the-scenes content

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree")
- External links (suppressed reach)
- Overly promotional content
- Posting without engagement strategy

### Facebook Groups Strategy

Groups receive 5-10x more organic reach than Pages:
1. Create niche community around your topic
2. Post valuable content daily
3. Encourage member discussions
4. Use Units for structured content
5. Host regular Lives in the Group

### Posting Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Best Times |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Page Posts | 1-2x per day | 1-4pm |
| Reels | 3-5x per week | Any time |
| Stories | 3-5x per day | Throughout day |
| Lives | 1-2x per week | Peak hours |
| Group Posts | 1-3x per day | Varies by group |
