# Founder Story Generator Framework

**Version:** 1.0  
**Created:** 18 January 2026  
**Purpose:** Generate compelling, business-centric origin narratives for investor pitches and stakeholder engagement

---

## Overview

The Founder Story Generator creates emotionally resonant narratives that explain **why a business exists** rather than focusing on the founder's personal journey. This approach ensures:

- Any founder can authentically present the story
- No fabrication of personal history required
- Emotional resonance comes from the problem and solution
- Universal applicability across diverse founding teams

---

## The Three-Act Structure

### Act 1: The Problem That Demanded a Solution

**Focus:** What observable market failure or gap exists?

**Key Questions:**
1. What frustration do customers/users experience daily?
2. What inefficiency costs businesses money or time?
3. What human need is being unmet or poorly served?
4. What industry assumption is fundamentally flawed?

**Narrative Template:**
> "Every day, [target audience] struggles with [specific problem]. Despite [existing solutions], they still face [ongoing pain point]. This costs them [quantifiable impact: time, money, opportunity, wellbeing]."

---

### Act 2: The Insight That Changed Everything

**Focus:** What revelation made the solution obvious?

**Key Questions:**
1. What pattern became visible that others missed?
2. What technology, trend, or shift enabled a new approach?
3. What cross-industry insight could be applied here?
4. What did research or observation reveal?

**Narrative Template:**
> "We realized that [key insight]. Unlike traditional approaches that [old method], we saw that [new possibility]. This wasn't just an improvement—it was a fundamental rethinking of [core assumption]."

---

### Act 3: The Mission That Drives Us

**Focus:** What impact will success create?

**Key Questions:**
1. How will customers' lives improve?
2. What industry transformation will occur?
3. What broader societal benefit emerges?
4. What legacy will this business create?

**Narrative Template:**
> "Our mission is to [transformation statement]. When we succeed, [target audience] will [new reality]. This isn't just a business—it's [larger purpose statement]."

## SME Review Panel

Each story draft should be reviewed by the following AI SME perspectives:

| SME Role | Review Focus | Key Questions |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Marketing Strategist** | Emotional hooks and memorability | Does it create an emotional response? Will people remember it? |
| **Strategy Consultant** | Market positioning and credibility | Does it position the business uniquely? Is it believable? |
| **Investor Relations** | Investment appeal | Does it signal opportunity? Does it reduce perceived risk? |
| **Behavioral Psychologist** | Human motivation | Does it tap into universal human needs? Is it authentic? |
| **Storyteller/Creative** | Narrative flow | Is it compelling? Does it build tension and resolution? |

## Story Generation Process

### Step 1: Problem Articulation
- Define the problem in customer's language
- Quantify the impact where possible
- Identify emotional pain (frustration, fear, wasted potential)

### Step 2: Insight Development
- Connect the problem to a unique observation
- Frame the insight as a revelation, not just an idea
- Show why existing solutions miss this insight

### Step 3: Mission Crystallization
- Elevate from product to purpose
- Connect individual benefit to broader impact
- Create a rallying cry that inspires action

### Step 4: SME Review
- Run through each SME perspective
- Refine based on feedback
- Ensure authenticity and credibility

### Step 5: Format for Context
- Investor pitch (90 seconds)
- Website "About" page
- Press release
- Team alignment document

---

## Output Formats

### 1. The Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)
> "[Problem statement]. We built [solution] because [insight]. Our mission: [purpose]."

### 2. The Investor Narrative (2 minutes)
Full three-act structure with supporting data points and market context.

### 3. The Website Story (300 words)
Engaging narrative for "About Us" or "Our Story" pages.

### 4. The Press Hook (1 sentence)
Quotable statement for media coverage.

---

## Quality Criteria

A successful founder story must pass these tests:

| Test | Question | Pass Criteria |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Authenticity** | Could any founder on the team deliver this? | Yes, without lying |
| **Memorability** | Can someone retell it after one hearing? | Key points stick |
| **Emotion** | Does it create feeling, not just understanding? | Evokes response |
| **Credibility** | Does it sound believable? | No red flags |
| **Differentiation** | Could a competitor tell the same story? | No, it's unique |
| **Action** | Does it inspire next steps? | Creates urgency |
