## Expert Profile Structure

Each AI SME is defined by a comprehensive profile containing the following elements:

**Identity Components**
- Unique identifier and name
- Category classification
- Composite inspiration sources (2-4 real-world thought leaders)
- Specialization areas

**Capability Metrics**
- Performance score (historical effectiveness rating)
- Project count (experience indicator)
- Strengths (top 3 capability areas)
- Weaknesses (known limitations)

**Behavioral Configuration**
- Thinking style (analytical, creative, strategic, etc.)
- Communication approach
- Decision-making framework
- Risk tolerance profile

## Team Assembly Protocol

When assembling an SME team for a project, follow this structured approach:

**Step 1: Requirements Analysis**
Identify the expertise domains required for the project. Consider both primary needs (core deliverables) and secondary needs (review, validation, edge cases).

**Step 2: Expert Selection**
Browse available experts by category or search by specialization. Review expert profiles to assess fit based on strengths, thinking style, and historical performance.

**Step 3: Team Composition**
Assemble a balanced team that covers all required domains while ensuring diversity of perspective. Typical team sizes range from 3-7 experts depending on project complexity.

**Step 4: Role Assignment**
Assign specific responsibilities to each team member. Define primary contributors, reviewers, and subject matter consultants.

**Step 5: Briefing**
Provide the assembled team with project context, objectives, constraints, and success criteria. Ensure all experts understand their role and the overall mission.

## Feedback and Learning System

The SME framework incorporates a continuous learning system that improves expert performance over time:

**Feedback Collection**
After each engagement, the Chief of Staff provides structured feedback on expert performance. This includes accuracy ratings, communication effectiveness, and value contribution.

**Pattern Recognition**
The system analyzes feedback patterns to identify consistent strengths and weaknesses for each expert. This informs future team assembly decisions and prompting strategies.

**Optimization**
The Chief of Staff maintains a knowledge base of optimal prompting approaches for each expert, refined through experience. This ensures maximum effectiveness in future engagements.

---

## Integration with Chief of Staff

The Chief of Staff serves as the orchestrator of SME teams, responsible for:

- Selecting appropriate experts for each project
- Coordinating team activities and communications
- Providing feedback and performance assessments
- Maintaining the SME knowledge base
- Optimizing prompting strategies

All SME interactions flow through the Chief of Staff to ensure quality control and consistent application of CEPHO standards.
