# Expert Team Templates

## The 12-Person Team Framework

Every significant project benefits from a diverse team of 12 experts. This framework ensures comprehensive coverage across domain expertise, cultural perspectives, target audience voices, and delivery capabilities.

---

## Core Team Structure

The 12-person team follows a consistent structure regardless of project type. Six roles are fixed, while six roles vary based on project domain.

### Fixed Roles (6 positions)

| Position | Role | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | Project Lead | Single point of accountability, synthesis, coordination |
| 2 | GCC Perspective Expert | Local cultural and business context |
| 3 | Western Perspective Expert | Global benchmarks and practices |
| 4 | Target Audience Voice (Female) | Reality check from intended user perspective |
| 5 | Target Audience Voice (Male) | Reality check from intended user perspective |
| 6 | Digital Twin (Chief of Staff) | User alignment, preference matching, coordination |

### Variable Roles (6 positions)

| Position | Role Type | Selected Based On |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| 7 | Domain Expert 1 | Primary project domain |
| 8 | Domain Expert 2 | Secondary project domain |
| 9 | Behavioural/Psychology Expert | Engagement and motivation design |
| 10 | Storytelling/Narrative Expert | Messaging and communication |
| 11 | Visual/Design Expert | Presentation and materials |
| 12 | Strategy/Framework Expert | Structure and methodology |

## Team Assembly Process

### Step 1: Project Classification

Analyze the project brief to determine the primary domain. This selects the base template.

### Step 2: Audience Identification

Identify the target audience to select appropriate Gen Z or demographic voices. For GCC projects, use GCC Gen Z voices. For Western projects, use Western Gen Z voices. For cross-cultural projects, include both.

### Step 3: Specialist Selection

Based on specific project requirements, select domain experts from the 290+ available. Consider performance scores and past success on similar projects.

### Step 4: Project Lead Assignment

Assign the most relevant domain expert as Project Lead. This person coordinates the team and synthesizes outputs.

### Step 5: Digital Twin Integration

The Digital Twin (Chief of Staff) is always included. It ensures alignment with user preferences and past decisions.

---

## Team Coordination

### Communication Flow

The Project Lead coordinates all expert inputs. Each expert provides their domain perspective. The Digital Twin ensures user alignment throughout.

### Synthesis Process

Individual expert inputs are synthesized by the Project Lead into a coherent recommendation. The Digital Twin reviews for user preference alignment before presentation.

### Quality Gates

Before delivery, outputs pass through:

1. Domain expert review (accuracy)
2. Storytelling expert review (narrative)
3. Visual expert review (presentation)
4. Digital Twin review (user alignment)

---

## Custom Team Assembly

For projects that do not fit standard templates, the system can assemble a custom team.

**Required inputs:**

1. Project domain (primary and secondary)
2. Target audience demographics
3. Geographic focus (GCC, Western, Global)
4. Deliverable format (presentation, report, plan)
5. Special requirements (regulatory, technical, creative)

**Assembly logic:**

The system matches project requirements to expert specialties, ensuring coverage of domain expertise, cultural perspectives, audience voices, and delivery capabilities.
