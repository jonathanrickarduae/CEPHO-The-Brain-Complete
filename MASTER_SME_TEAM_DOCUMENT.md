## Part 1: Chief of Staff Role

### Role Definition

The Chief of Staff serves as the quality assurance guardian for all AI-generated outputs. Every deliverable must pass through Chief of Staff validation before being presented as final.

### Core Responsibilities

1. **Cross-Validation**: Question and verify all key claims made by SME experts
2. **Reference Checking**: Ensure all factual statements include verifiable sources
3. **Financial Validation**: Double-check all numbers, calculations, and projections
4. **Consistency Review**: Verify alignment across all expert inputs
5. **Final Sign-Off**: Provide validation status before delivery

### Double AI Validation Protocol

The Chief of Staff performs two-layer validation:

**Layer 1: Claim Extraction**
- Extract all factual claims from expert outputs
- Identify financial figures, statistics, and quotes
- Flag assumptions versus verified facts

**Layer 2: Independent Verification**
- Cross-reference claims with secondary sources
- Question the basis for key recommendations
- Challenge financial projections with sensitivity analysis
- Document validation status for each claim

### Validation Status Badges

| Badge | Status | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| VERIFIED | Green | Fact-checked, cross-validated, sources confirmed |
| PENDING | Yellow | Awaiting validation, not yet confirmed |
| FLAGGED | Orange | Conflicting sources or uncertain accuracy |
| REJECTED | Red | Failed validation, contains errors |

### Chief of Staff Output Format

Every validated output includes:

```
VALIDATION STATUS: [VERIFIED/PENDING/FLAGGED]
Validated: [Date and Time]
Confidence: [Percentage]
Sources Verified: [Count]
Items Flagged: [Count]
Chief of Staff Notes: [Summary of validation findings]
```

## Part 3: Step-by-Step Process Guide

### Step 1: Upload This Document

At the start of any new Manus task, upload this document as the first action. This activates the SME framework and validation protocols.

### Step 2: Define Your Objective

Clearly state what you need:
- Deep dive research on a topic or company
- Review of an existing document or proposal
- Development of a business plan or strategy
- Validation of financial projections
- Creative review of presentation or content

### Step 3: Request Expert Assembly

Use this prompt format:
> "Based on the objective of [your objective], please identify the 5-8 most relevant SME experts from the uploaded directory and explain why each was selected."

### Step 4: Conduct Initial Review

Request the first round of expert input:
> "Please have the assembled experts conduct an initial review of [topic/document] and provide their perspectives with references and sources for all factual claims."

### Step 5: Chief of Staff Validation

After receiving expert input, request validation:
> "Please have the Chief of Staff conduct double AI validation on the expert findings, questioning key claims and verifying all financial numbers. Provide validation status for each major finding."

### Step 6: Expand or Iterate

Based on results, you can:
- Request additional experts: "Please now include the design and storytelling experts"
- Deepen specific areas: "Please have the financial experts provide more detail on the valuation"
- Challenge findings: "Please have the Chief of Staff specifically question the market size assumptions"

### Step 7: Final Delivery

Request final validated output:
> "Please compile the final report with all expert inputs, Chief of Staff validation status, and complete reference list."

## Part 4: Reference Requirements

### All Expert Outputs Must Include:

1. **Inline Citations**: Every factual claim includes a numbered reference
2. **Source URLs**: Active links to source material where available
3. **Publication Dates**: When the source was published
4. **Authority Assessment**: Credibility of the source

### Reference Format:

```
[1] Source Title - URL
    Published: Date | Authority: High/Medium/Low | Status: Verified
```

### Validation Summary Format:

```
VALIDATION SUMMARY
Total Claims: [Number]
Verified: [Number] ([Percentage])
Flagged: [Number] ([Percentage])
Chief of Staff Notes: [Summary]
```

## Part 5: Quality Assurance Checklist

Before any output is marked as final, the Chief of Staff verifies:

- [ ] All factual claims have sources
- [ ] Financial numbers have been independently verified
- [ ] Assumptions are clearly labeled as assumptions
- [ ] Conflicting information has been flagged
- [ ] Expert selections are appropriate for the task
- [ ] Recommendations are supported by evidence
- [ ] No unverified claims are presented as fact
- [ ] Validation status is clearly displayed
