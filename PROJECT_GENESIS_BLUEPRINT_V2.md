# Project Genesis Blueprint V2

## Complete Workflow: Voice Note to Expert-Driven Deliverable

This blueprint captures the complete, proven workflow for transforming a stakeholder voice note into a fully researched, expert-driven deliverable with project management, milestone tracking, and continuous quality assurance.

---

## The Complete Process Flow

```
Voice Note Intake
       ↓
Scope Alignment Playback (3-min voice note)
       ↓
Project Genesis Wizard
       ↓
Project Plan & Gantt Chart Creation
       ↓
Expert Team Assembly (12-15 person team)
       ↓
Initial Research Phase
       ↓
Milestone 1: Structure & Framework → Voice Update + Status Report
       ↓
Deep Research (50 companies benchmark) → Background Processing
       ↓
Milestone 2: Content Development → Voice Update + Status Report
       ↓
Idea Scoring & Ranking by Leadership Team
       ↓
Milestone 3: Quality Review & Refinement → Voice Update + Status Report
       ↓
Final Deliverable with Sign-off
       ↓
Project Archive & Lessons Learned
```

---

## Phase 1: Voice Note Intake

The journey begins with a voice note. A stakeholder records their vision, requirements, and context in a 2-5 minute voice message.

**What happens:**

The voice note is transcribed using speech-to-text technology. The transcription is analyzed to extract key project parameters that pre-populate the Project Genesis wizard.

**Pre-population targets:**

| Wizard Question | Extracted From Voice Note |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| What are we trying to achieve? | Primary objective statements |
| Who is this for? | Target audience mentions |
| Internal or external project? | Context clues about stakeholders |
| Timeline expectations | Deadline or urgency mentions |
| Success criteria | Outcome descriptions (e.g., "award-winning", "world-class") |
| Budget constraints | Any cost or resource mentions |
| Deliverable format | Presentation, report, plan mentions |

---

## Phase 2: Scope Alignment Playback

Once the project is set up, the system generates a 3-minute voice note that plays back the scope to the project owner.

**Voice Note Script Structure:**

> "This is your scope alignment for [Project Name]. Based on your initial brief, here's what we're setting out to achieve:
> 
> **Objective:** [Primary goal in clear terms]
> 
> **Target Audience:** [Who this is for]
> 
> **Success Criteria:** [What "done" looks like]
> 
> **Timeline:** [When each phase will complete]
> 
> **Key Deliverables:** [What you'll receive]
> 
> Please confirm this scope is correct, or provide any adjustments needed before we proceed."

**Purpose:**

This ensures alignment before work begins. The project owner can confirm or adjust the scope through a voice note response.

---

## Phase 3: Project Plan & Gantt Chart

Once scope is confirmed, the system generates a formal project plan with milestones and a Gantt chart.

**Project Plan Components:**

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Executive Summary | One-paragraph project overview |
| Objectives | Specific, measurable goals |
| Scope | What's included and excluded |
| Milestones | Key checkpoints with dates |
| Deliverables | What will be produced |
| Team | Expert team composition |
| Timeline | Gantt chart visualization |
| Risks | Potential issues and mitigations |
| Success Criteria | How success will be measured |

**Gantt Chart Structure:**

```
Week 1          Week 2          Week 3          Week 4
|----M1----|
            |----M2----|
                        |----M3----|
                                    |--Final--|

M1: Structure & Framework
M2: Content Development  
M3: Quality Review
Final: Deliverable Sign-off
```

**Update Cadence:**

The project plan and Gantt chart are updated after each milestone completion and sent to the project owner.

---

## Phase 4: Expert Team Assembly

The system assembles a 12-15 person expert team based on project requirements.

**Core Team Structure (12 positions):**

| Position | Role | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | Project Lead | Coordination, synthesis, accountability |
| 2 | GCC Perspective Expert | Local cultural and business context |
| 3 | Western Perspective Expert | Global benchmarks and practices |
| 4 | Target Audience Voice (Female) | Reality check from user perspective |
| 5 | Target Audience Voice (Male) | Reality check from user perspective |
| 6 | Digital Twin (Chief of Staff) | User alignment, preference matching |
| 7 | Domain Expert 1 | Primary project domain |
| 8 | Domain Expert 2 | Secondary project domain |
| 9 | Cultural Awareness Expert | Regional sensitivity review |
| 10 | Storytelling Expert | Narrative and messaging |
| 11 | Visual/Design Expert | Presentation and materials |
| 12 | Strategy/Framework Expert | Structure and methodology |

**Extended Team (3 additional positions for complex projects):**

| Position | Role | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| 13 | Quality Assurance Lead | Continuous review and scoring |
| 14 | Deep Research Specialist | Background benchmarking |
| 15 | Innovation Scout | Future ideas and possibilities |

---

## Phase 5: Milestone-Based Voice Updates

After each milestone is completed and signed off, the system generates a voice note update for the project owner.

**Voice Update Structure:**

> "This is your milestone update for [Project Name].
> 
> **Milestone Completed:** [Milestone Name]
> 
> **What Was Achieved:** [Summary of work completed]
> 
> **Key Decisions Made:** [Important choices and rationale]
> 
> **Ideas Captured:** [New ideas logged for future consideration]
> 
> **Next Milestone:** [What's coming next]
> 
> **Updated Timeline:** [Any changes to the schedule]
> 
> The updated project status report is attached for your review."

**Accompanying Documents:**

Each voice update is accompanied by:

1. Updated Project Status Report (PDF)
2. Updated Gantt Chart
3. Milestone Deliverables
4. Ideas Log (new ideas captured during this phase)

---

## Phase 6: Deep Research Integration

During project execution, deep research runs in the background to inform the team.

**Deep Research Process:**

The system conducts benchmarking research, such as analyzing how 50 leading companies approach the same challenge. This research runs asynchronously while the main project progresses.

**Research Output:**

| Component | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| Company Profiles | Brief overview of each benchmarked company |
| Best Practices | Common successful approaches |
| Innovation Examples | Unique or creative solutions |
| Lessons Learned | What to avoid based on failures |
| Applicability Assessment | Which ideas fit this project context |

**Integration with Main Project:**

Research findings are fed to the leadership team for review. Relevant ideas are scored and considered for inclusion in the project deliverables.

---

## Phase 7: Idea Scoring & Ranking System

As ideas emerge from the expert team and deep research, they are scored and ranked by the leadership team.

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Strategic Fit | 25% | Alignment with project objectives |
| Cultural Appropriateness | 20% | Suitability for target context (e.g., GCC) |
| Feasibility | 20% | Can it be implemented within constraints? |
| Impact | 20% | Expected value or benefit |
| Cost/Time | 15% | Resource requirements |

**Scoring Process:**

Each idea is scored by the 15-person leadership team. Scores are aggregated and ranked. The project owner reviews the top-ranked ideas for final decision.

**Idea Categories:**

| Category | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Include Now | Add to current deliverable |
| Consider Later | Log for future phases |
| Reject | Not suitable (with rationale) |

**Cultural Review Gate:**

The Cultural Awareness Expert has veto power on ideas that may not be appropriate for the target context. For example, a leadership board concept might be flagged as unsuitable for the Middle East context.

---

## Phase 8: Quality Assurance

The core team continuously reviews work in progress, providing quality assurance throughout the project.

**QA Checkpoints:**

| Checkpoint | Timing | Focus |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Scope Alignment | After intake | Correct understanding of requirements |
| Structure Review | After M1 | Framework and approach validation |
| Content Review | After M2 | Accuracy and completeness |
| Final Review | Before sign-off | Overall quality and polish |

**QA Team Composition:**

The QA function is performed by the core team members, each reviewing from their perspective:

- Project Lead: Overall coherence
- Domain Experts: Technical accuracy
- Cultural Expert: Regional appropriateness
- Storytelling Expert: Narrative flow
- Visual Expert: Design quality
- Digital Twin: User preference alignment

---

## Phase 9: Project Status Reports

Formal project status reports are generated at each milestone and sent to the project owner.

**Status Report Structure:**

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Project Overview | Name, objective, current phase |
| Progress Summary | What's been completed |
| Milestone Status | On track / At risk / Delayed |
| Key Decisions | Important choices made |
| Issues & Risks | Current challenges and mitigations |
| Ideas Log | New ideas captured this period |
| Next Steps | What's coming next |
| Updated Timeline | Gantt chart with progress |
| Team Activity | Expert contributions this period |

**Delivery Method:**

1. Voice note summary (2-3 minutes)
2. PDF status report
3. Updated Gantt chart
4. Milestone deliverables (if applicable)

---

## Phase 10: Final Deliverable & Sign-off

The project concludes with final deliverable presentation and sign-off.

**Final Package:**

| Component | Format |
|-----------|--------|
| Primary Deliverable | Presentation, Report, or Plan |
| Executive Summary | 1-page overview |
| Project Completion Report | Full project documentation |
| Ideas Archive | All ideas captured (included and deferred) |
| Lessons Learned | What worked, what to improve |
| Voice Summary | 5-minute project completion briefing |

**Sign-off Process:**

1. Final deliverable presented to project owner
2. Review period with feedback option
3. Revisions if needed
4. Formal sign-off confirmation
5. Project archived with full documentation

---

## The Ideas Capture System

Throughout the project, ideas are captured, scored, and logged for current or future use.

**Ideas Log Structure:**

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Idea ID | Unique identifier |
| Source | Which expert or research source |
| Description | What the idea is |
| Rationale | Why it was suggested |
| Scores | Leadership team scores |
| Status | Include / Consider Later / Reject |
| Cultural Flag | Any cultural concerns |
| Cost Estimate | If applicable |
| Time Estimate | If applicable |

**Future Ideas Repository:**

Ideas marked "Consider Later" are stored in a project-specific repository for future phases or related projects.

## Budget & Timeline Considerations

The Project Genesis wizard captures budget and timeline constraints that influence the entire workflow.

**Budget Impact:**

| Budget Level | Impact |
|--------------|--------|
| No budget specified | Full exploration, all ideas considered |
| Limited budget | Prioritize high-impact, low-cost ideas |
| Fixed budget | Strict cost-benefit analysis on all ideas |

**Timeline Impact:**

| Timeline | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Urgent (< 1 week) | Streamlined process, core team only |
| Standard (2-4 weeks) | Full process with deep research |
| Extended (> 4 weeks) | Comprehensive exploration, multiple iterations |

## Team Expansion: From 12 to 15

For complex projects, the team expands from 12 to 15 members.

**When to Expand:**

- Projects with significant cultural complexity
- Projects requiring extensive benchmarking
- Projects with innovation requirements
- Projects with multiple stakeholder groups

**Additional Roles:**

| Role | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Quality Assurance Lead | Dedicated QA oversight |
| Deep Research Specialist | Background benchmarking |
| Innovation Scout | Future possibilities |

| Status Reports | All milestone reports |
| Gantt Charts | Version history of timeline |

2. Learning for future projects
