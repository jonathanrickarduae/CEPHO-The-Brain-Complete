# Go-To-Market Strategy
Phase: 3
Status: Consolidated Draft

## 1. Go-to-Market Motion

The go-to-market (GTM) strategy is a hybrid model that combines **product-led growth (PLG)** for the lower tiers with a **solution-selling sales motion** for the enterprise tier.

-   **PLG (Starter & Professional):** A free trial will drive user acquisition and allow the product to demonstrate its value directly. The onboarding flow will be critical for converting trial users to paid customers.
-   **Sales-Led (Enterprise):** A targeted outbound sales effort will focus on large enterprises with clear QMS pain points. The sales process will be value-based, focusing on the ROI of the 100% Optimization Framework and the strategic value of the Persephone-AI board.

## 2. Customer Acquisition Channels

The customer acquisition strategy is multi-channel, with a strong emphasis on content and partnerships to drive high-quality, low-cost leads [5].

| Channel | Strategy | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Content-Led Growth** | Publish thought leadership content (blog posts, whitepapers, webinars) showcasing the expertise of the AI SME panel. | Drives high-quality inbound leads, builds brand authority, and has the lowest long-term CAC. |
| **Partner/Referral Program** | Build partnerships with QMS vendors, consulting firms, and industry associations. | Leverages existing relationships and provides warm introductions to target accounts. Highly efficient CAC. |
| **Targeted Outbound Sales** | Focus outbound efforts on enterprise accounts in regulated industries (e.g., life sciences, manufacturing) with known QMS needs. | Directly targets the most lucrative customer segment with a tailored value proposition. |
| **Community Building** | Create and nurture a community around the 100% Optimization Framework and the future of AI in business. | Builds a long-term competitive moat, fosters customer loyalty, and generates organic growth. |
