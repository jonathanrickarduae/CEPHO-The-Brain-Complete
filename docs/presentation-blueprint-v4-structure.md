# Universal Presentation Blueprint v4.0 - Key Structure

## Part 1: Configuration Engine (Upfront Inputs)

### 1.1 Core Strategy & Narrative
- The Governing Thought (single overarching hypothesis)
- The Primary Goal (measurable action for audience)
- The Core Story (fundamental problem and importance)
- The Solution (unique and defensible approach)
- The Founder/Origin Story (compelling "why")

### 1.2 Audience & Persuasion
- Audience Persona (goals, pain points, habits)
- Persuasion Strategy (psychological principles)
  - Authority
  - Social Proof
  - Scarcity

### 1.3 Market & Financials
- Market Opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- The Moat (3-5 defensible advantages)
- Financial Projections (3-5 year, Revenue, EBITDA)
- The Ask (capital amount, milestones)

### 1.4 Project Management & Communications
- Risk Register (top 5 risks + mitigation)
- Stakeholder Communications Plan
- Select External Peer Reviewers (Asset Manager, Investment Bank, VC, Family Office)

## Part 3: The Agile Sprint Process

### Sprint 1: Strategy & Structure
- **Goal:** Finalize strategic foundation and narrative structure
- **Activities:** Create Governing Thought, Audience Persona, Narrative Flow Map, select Deck Architecture
- **Team:** Core Team, Industry Analyst
- **QA Gates:** QA 0 (Thesis Alignment), QA 1 (Structure & Moat)

### Sprint 2: Content & Data
- **Goal:** Draft all slide content and verify all data
- **Activities:** Write all text, create charts, verify all claims and sources
- **Team:** Adds Copywriter, SME (Technical), SME (Finance)
- **QA Gates:** QA 2 (Narrative & Cognitive Ease), QA 3 (Technical Verification)

### Sprint 3: Design & ESG
- **Goal:** Develop visual identity and ESG narrative
- **Activities:** Select imagery (enforcing 25% rule), apply branding, assess ESG impact
- **Team:** Adds Designer, ESG Analyst
- **QA Gates:** QA 4 (ESG), QA 5 (Visual & Data Viz)

### Sprint 4: External Validation & Delivery Prep
- **Goal:** Validate with external perspectives and prepare for delivery
- **Activities:** Conduct peer reviews, write speaker notes, create Q&A document
- **Team:** Adds External Peer Reviewers, Script Writer
- **QA Gates:** QA 6 (External Peer Review), QA 7 (Script & Delivery)

### Sprint 5: Finalization & Handoff
- **Goal:** Gain final client approval and deliver project
- **Activities:** Conduct final client review, package all deliverables
- **Team:** Core Team, Client
- **QA Gates:** QA 8 (Client Sign-Off)

### Sprint 6: Post-Mortem & Analytics
- **Goal:** Analyze effectiveness and improve blueprint
- **Activities:** Track follow-up rates, gather audience feedback, update playbook
- **Team:** Core Team
- **QA Gates:** None (Analysis Phase)

## Part 4: The Automation Pathway

- **Automated Configuration:** Auto-generate project brief, risk register, Digital Twin team selection
- **Automated Drafting:** Generate first draft based on architecture and narrative inputs
- **Automated QA Routing:** Assign review tasks to correct Digital Twins at sprint start
- **Predictive Feedback:** Analyze historical QA data to predict investor questions and suggest improvements
