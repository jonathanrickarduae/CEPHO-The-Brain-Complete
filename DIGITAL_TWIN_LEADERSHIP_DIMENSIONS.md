**Version:** 1.0

## Instructions

This assessment measures your alignment with seven leadership thinking styles. For each statement, rate how strongly it reflects your natural approach on a scale of 1 to 10.

**Format:** Answer as L1-7, L2-8, etc.

---

## Section L: Leadership Thinking Dimensions (70 Questions)

### MIT Graduate Thinking (L1-L10)

L1. I prefer to see data and metrics before making decisions.

L2. I naturally break complex problems into component parts.

L3. I think in terms of systems, inputs, outputs, and feedback loops.

L4. I want to understand the technical details, not just the summary.

L5. I value precision and accuracy over speed.

L6. I test assumptions with experiments rather than relying on intuition.

L7. I look for ways to optimize and improve efficiency.

L8. I am comfortable with mathematical models and quantitative analysis.

L9. I prefer solutions that are scalable and repeatable.

L10. I believe most problems have an engineering solution.

---

### Harvard Graduate Thinking (L11-L20)

L11. I think about competitive advantage in every business situation.

L12. I consider all stakeholders and their interests before deciding.

L13. I evaluate decisions based on long term value creation.

L14. I use case studies and precedents to inform my thinking.

L15. I am comfortable with strategic ambiguity and trade offs.

L16. I think about market positioning and differentiation.

L17. I consider the governance and organizational implications of decisions.

L18. I value building relationships and networks for business success.

L19. I think about how decisions affect the overall business portfolio.

L20. I prefer structured debate and diverse perspectives before concluding.

---

### NASA Scientist Thinking (L21-L30)

L21. I plan for worst case scenarios and have contingency plans.

L22. I document processes thoroughly for repeatability.

L23. I identify single points of failure and build redundancy.

L24. I believe in rigorous testing before implementation.

L25. I am uncomfortable with unnecessary risk.

L26. I prefer methodical, step by step approaches.

L27. I review and learn from failures systematically.

L28. I insist on quality assurance and verification.

L29. I think about what could go wrong before what could go right.

L30. I value precision and attention to detail in execution.

---

### McKinsey Consultant Thinking (L31-L40)

L31. I structure problems using frameworks and models.

L32. I develop hypotheses early and test them systematically.

L33. I present recommendations with the answer first.

L34. I ensure my analysis is MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive).

L35. I focus on the "so what" and actionable insights.

L36. I am comfortable presenting to senior executives.

L37. I use 80/20 thinking to focus on what matters most.

L38. I benchmark against best practices and competitors.

L39. I create clear, visual presentations of complex information.

L40. I drive toward three to five key recommendations.

L41. I think about capital preservation as much as growth.

L42. I evaluate investments on risk adjusted returns.

L43. I consider tax implications in financial decisions.

L44. I think about liquidity and access to capital.

L45. I consider multi generational wealth implications.

L46. I diversify to manage concentration risk.

L47. I am skeptical of investments I do not fully understand.

L48. I think about the opportunity cost of every allocation.

L49. I prefer recurring, predictable income streams.

L50. I consider estate and succession planning in decisions.

---

### CEO Thinking (L51-L60)

L51. I take ownership and accountability for outcomes.

L52. I balance short term performance with long term sustainability.

L53. I think about organizational capacity and talent.

L54. I make decisions even with incomplete information.

L55. I consider the cultural implications of decisions.

L56. I communicate vision and align teams around goals.

L57. I allocate resources based on strategic priorities.

L58. I hold people accountable for results.

L59. I consider what the board and investors expect.

L60. I think about legacy and the organization I am building.

---

### Visionary Entrepreneur Thinking (L61-L70)

L61. I see opportunities where others see obstacles.

L62. I challenge assumptions and conventional wisdom.

L63. I think about 10x improvements, not incremental gains.

L64. I move quickly and iterate based on feedback.

L65. I am comfortable with ambiguity and uncertainty.

L66. I think about where markets are heading, not where they are.

L67. I am willing to fail in pursuit of breakthrough success.

L68. I look for ways to disrupt existing industries.

L69. I trust my instincts when data is limited.

L70. I believe in creating new value rather than capturing existing value.

**Scores 5-7:** This is a supporting thinking style. The Digital Twin should incorporate this perspective when relevant.
