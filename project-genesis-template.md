# Project Genesis Template Structure
## Based on Boundless Business Analysis Deep Dive

### Document Structure (79 pages, 6 Parts)

---

## Part 1: Introduction & Opportunity
- **Section A: Executive Summary**
  - Overview (what is this business)
  - The Opportunity (market size, unit economics)
  - Critical Issues Requiring Resolution
  - Recommendations (go/no-go conditions)
  - Conclusion

- **Section A2: What Makes [Company] Different**
  - Key Differentiators table (15+ items)
  - Differentiator | Description | Competitive Advantage

- **Section B: Market Segmentation & Revenue Opportunity**
  - B2B Market Verticals Deep Dive
  - For each vertical: Overview, Target, Data Usage, Scale Potential, Churn Risk, Sales Angle

- **Section B4: Financial Model & Valuation**
  - Revenue projections
  - EBITDA margins
  - DCF valuation range

---

## Part 2: The Product
- **Section C: The Product - Claims vs Reality**
  - What they say vs what's actually true
  - Technical validation needed

- **Section D: The App Development Pathway**
  - Product roadmap
  - MVP vs full product

---

## Part 3: Market & Competitive Analysis
- **Section E: Competitive & Market Landscape**
  - Direct competitors
  - Indirect competitors
  - Market positioning

- **Section F: Geographic Market Analysis**
  - Region-specific opportunities
  - Regulatory considerations per market

- **Section G: Industry Deep Dives**
  - Specific sector analysis

- **Section H: Intelligence & Surveillance Considerations**
  - Security/privacy implications

---

## Part 4: Due Diligence & Risk
- **Section I: Due Diligence on Key People**
  - Founder background checks
  - Corporate history
  - Red flags

- **Section J: Case Studies**
  - Failed similar businesses
  - Lessons learned

- **Section K: Risk Register (30 Evidence-Based Risks)**
  - K.1 Supplier & Partner Risks (R01-R10)
  - K.2 Product & Technology Risks (R11-R15)
  - K.3 Regulatory & Legal Risks (R16-R20)
  - K.4 Market & Financial Risks (R21-R25)
  - K.5 Governance & Founder Risks (R26-R30)
  
  Each risk: ID | Risk | Probability | Impact | Evidence | Mitigation

---

## Part 5: Corporate Structure & Protection
- **Section L: Recommended Corporate & Ownership Structure**
  - Jurisdiction analysis (Cayman, BVI, Jersey, etc.)
  - Comparison matrix: Setup Cost, Annual Fees, Formation Time, Tax, Reputation, Privacy
  - Ghost owner options
  - Nominee directors
  - Trust structures

- **Section M: Mitigation Framework**
  - Ironclad contract provisions
  - Price caps, termination rights
  - Data ownership clauses
  - Non-compete provisions

- **Section N: Legal Considerations**
  - Regulatory compliance per market
  - GDPR, data protection
  - Telecommunications licensing

---

## Part 6: Execution & Exit
- **Section O: Launch Roadmap (8 Week)**
  - Week-by-week action plan
  - Milestones and deliverables

- **Section P: Exit Strategy Analysis**
  - Potential acquirers
  - Valuation multiples
  - Timeline to exit

- **Section Q: Conclusion & Final Recommendations**
  - Go/no-go decision
  - Conditions for proceeding
  - Priority actions

- **Section S: Numbers Validation & Sources**
  - Data sources
  - Assumption validation

- **Section R: References**
  - All sources cited

---

## Appendices
- **Appendix A: Detailed Customer Channel Breakdowns**
- **Appendix B: Financial & Operational Details**
- **Appendix C: Data Science & Analytics Options**

---

## Project Genesis Quick-Fire Questions

### Phase 1: Basic Info
1. Company/Project name?
2. One-line description?
3. Industry/sector?
4. Stage (idea/MVP/revenue)?

### Phase 2: People
5. Founder names and roles?
6. Founder emails?
7. Shareholding split?
8. Key advisors/partners?

### Phase 3: Business
9. Target market?
10. Revenue model?
11. Current revenue (if any)?
12. Funding target?
13. Use of funds?

### Phase 4: Legal
14. Jurisdiction preference?
15. Existing company registered?
16. Any existing contracts/agreements?

### Phase 5: Due Diligence Flags
17. Any known risks/concerns?
18. Supplier dependencies?
19. Regulatory requirements?

## Explainer Video Script Structure (2-3 mins)

1. **Opening (15s)**: What is [Company]?
2. **Problem (20s)**: What problem does it solve?
3. **Solution (30s)**: How does it work?
4. **Market (20s)**: How big is the opportunity?
5. **Moat (20s)**: Why can't competitors copy this?
6. **Traction (15s)**: What's been achieved?
7. **Team (15s)**: Who's behind it?
8. **Ask (15s)**: What's needed to proceed?
9. **Close (10s)**: Next steps
